There are always two things I try to keep in mind when taking a photo: context and memory.


I believe that environments are as significant to a portrait as the individuals that are depicted in them, not only in regard to the personality of the portrait itself, but also in contextualizing the identity of the subject and the space they occupy in a landscape.


Memory is the second dominant theme throughout my photos. My intention is to capture an image that reflects how a moment will be remembered rather than a precise depiction of the event. A meaningful photo will reinforce the half-remembered bias of a beautiful memory through color and light. It is my goal every time I look through the lens of my camera to take a meaningful photo.
